## 1. Executive Summary

This report presents a connected analysis of two years of retail sales performance across the business. Total sales reached ₹217M across 2024–2025, generating ₹33.3M in profit at a 15.4% overall margin. The business is growing — 2025 sales of ₹110.8M exceeded 2024's ₹106.2M by 4.3% — but that headline masks a more complex story beneath it.

The business is structurally dependent on one category — Technology — which alone generates 84% of total profit. Three customer segments are nearly equal in revenue, yet one carries a return rate 45% higher than the others. Deep discounting is silently destroying margin on 288 orders. And the category with the highest sales volume outside Technology — Furniture — is generating a profit margin of just 6.9%, making it the least efficient use of the business's capital and marketing investment.

The dashboard connects these findings into a single narrative: the business has a strong core, a concentration risk, a discounting problem, and a clear path to improvement.

---

## 2. What Is Performing Well

**Technology is the profit engine — and it is firing on all cylinders.**

When leadership views the Sales Trend chart alongside the Category Profitability view, the story is clear: Technology sub-categories — Copiers, Accessories, Phones, and Machines — dominate the top of the profit chart with margins of 18%, nearly three times the Furniture category's 6.9%. Copiers alone contribute ₹7.3M in profit. Technology accounts for ₹153.9M of total sales and ₹28M of profit — both the highest of any category by a significant margin.

**The South region is the strongest geography.**

The Regional Performance chart shows South generating ₹64.7M in sales and ₹10.0M in profit — more than either East or West by over ₹15M in sales. Combined with the Sales Trend, it is evident that South's strength is consistent rather than driven by a single strong month.

**Same Day shipping customers are the most valuable.**

The Shipping Performance view, read alongside the Customer Segment chart, reveals that Same Day shipping orders generate an average profit of ₹9,102 — 16% higher than Standard Class. These customers are likely less price-sensitive, less likely to request discounts, and more likely to be Corporate or high-value Home Office buyers. The business's most profitable customer behaviour is concentrated in this relatively small segment of 241 orders.

**Year-on-year growth is positive.**

Profit grew from ₹16.5M in 2024 to ₹16.8M in 2025 — a modest but consistent improvement. The business is not declining.

## 3. What Is Underperforming

**Furniture is generating revenue without generating returns.**

The Category Profitability view tells a stark story: Furniture generates ₹51.6M in sales — significant volume — but produces only ₹3.6M in profit at a 6.9% margin. Sub-categories like Tables (5.7% margin) and Bookcases (5.7% margin) are the worst performers in the entire business. When this is combined with the Return Analysis view — which shows Furniture's return rate at 7.67%, more than double Technology's 3.03% — the picture becomes clear: Furniture is consuming logistics, marketing, and inventory resources while delivering the lowest returns and the highest return rate.

**East and West regions are leaving revenue on the table.**

Despite having similar margin profiles to South and North (15.1%–15.6%), East and West each generate only ₹48.9M in sales — ₹15.8M less than South. The Sales Trend view shows no structural reason why these regions should be this far behind. This is a volume gap, not a profitability gap, suggesting that marketing investment or sales capacity in these regions is underweight relative to their potential.

**Standard Class — the dominant shipping choice — has the longest delays and lowest profit.**

Standard Class handles 58% of all orders (2,435) but delivers the lowest average profit per order (₹7,839) with the longest average delivery time (4.71 days). When customers choosing Standard Class are also receiving 21–30% discounts, these become the loss-making orders identified in the discount analysis.

## 4. What Risks Are Visible

**Risk 1 — Technology concentration.**
The single most significant risk visible in the dashboard is the business's dependence on Technology. With 84% of profit coming from one category, any disruption — supply chain delays, competitive pricing pressure, or a slowdown in corporate technology spending — would deliver an outsized blow to the business's financial performance. The Sales Trend shows no current signs of Technology declining, but the concentration itself is the risk.

**Risk 2 — Discount-driven profit destruction.**
The Discount vs Profit view reveals that 288 orders — 6.9% of all orders — are loss-making. The majority (204 orders) sit in the 21–30% discount band. This is not isolated exceptions — it is a systematic pattern. At an average loss of ₹1,601 per order, these 288 orders are collectively destroying approximately ₹461,000 in profit annually. If deep discounting is being used to win volume, it is winning volume at a net loss.

**Risk 3 — Home Office return rate.**
The Customer Segment view shows Home Office as the highest-revenue segment at ₹74.5M. But the Return Analysis reveals Home Office customers return products at a rate of 5.67% — significantly higher than Consumer (3.91%) and Corporate (4.00%). As Home Office grows, unchecked returns will erode the segment's net contribution to profit.

**Risk 4 — Furniture's structural weakness.**
Furniture's combination of low margin (6.9%), high return rate (7.67%), and flat sales trend makes it a structurally weak part of the business. It is not declining, but it is not improving either — and each unit of investment in Furniture delivers less than half the return of the same investment in Technology.

## 5. What Opportunities Are Visible

**Opportunity 1 — Grow East and West to match South.**
East and West have the same margin profile as South but generate ₹15.8M less in sales each. If East and West could be brought to South's sales level through targeted marketing investment or increased sales team capacity, total business revenue would increase by approximately ₹31.6M annually — without any change in product mix or pricing.

**Opportunity 2 — Shift Furniture investment to Technology.**
Technology sub-categories generate 18% margins. Furniture generates 6.9%. Reallocating a portion of Furniture's marketing and inventory budget to Technology — particularly Copiers and Accessories — would improve overall business margin immediately.

**Opportunity 3 — Eliminate loss-making discount orders.**
Capping discounts at 20% would eliminate the 204 orders in the 21–30% band that are the primary source of negative-profit transactions. If average profit per order in this band could be restored from -₹1,601 to breakeven, the business would recover approximately ₹327,000 in annual profit with no change in volume.

**Opportunity 4 — Promote Same Day shipping to high-value segments.**
Same Day shipping generates ₹9,102 average profit per order. If the proportion of Same Day orders grew from 6% to 10% of total volume through targeted upselling to Corporate and Home Office customers, and those orders retained their average profit profile, this would add approximately ₹1.6M in profit annually.

**Opportunity 5 — Reduce Furniture return rate.**
Bringing Furniture's return rate from 7.67% to match Office Supplies' 3.65% through better product information, expectation-setting, and post-purchase support would reduce returns by approximately 86 orders annually — recovering reverse logistics costs and protecting net revenue.

## 6. Recommended Business Actions

Based on what the dashboard reveals when the charts are read together, the following actions are recommended in priority order:

**Action 1 — Implement a 20% discount cap immediately.**
The data is unambiguous: discounts above 20% produce loss-making orders at scale. A policy change here has the fastest and most certain impact on profitability with no revenue investment required.

**Action 2 — Conduct a Furniture portfolio review.**
With low margins, high return rates, and flat growth, Furniture needs either a clear improvement plan or a managed reduction in investment. The review should ask: can Furniture margins be improved to 12%+ through repricing or cost reduction? If not, investment should be redirected to Technology.

**Action 3 — Develop a South replication playbook for East and West.**
South's sales advantage over East and West is a volume story, not a margin story. Understanding what drives South's volume — stronger campaigns, better store coverage, higher population density — and applying those tactics to East and West is the highest-potential revenue growth lever available.

**Action 4 — Launch a Home Office returns reduction programme.**
Home Office is the highest-revenue segment with the highest return risk. A targeted post-purchase experience improvement — product guides, setup support, proactive satisfaction outreach — can reduce the 5.67% return rate and protect this segment's net contribution.

**Action 5 — Prioritise Same Day and First Class shipping upselling.**
Given the profit premium of Same Day orders, sales and e-commerce teams should be incentivised to promote premium shipping at checkout — particularly for high-value Technology orders to Corporate customers.

## 7. Limitations of the Dashboard

**Limitation 1 — No cost breakdown by category.**
The dashboard shows profit and sales but not the underlying cost structure. It is not possible to determine whether Furniture's low margin is driven by high product cost, high logistics cost, or high return processing cost. A cost breakdown would sharpen the Furniture recommendation significantly.

**Limitation 2 — No campaign-level attribution.**
The `campaign_channel` field has 24 null values and is not prominently featured in the dashboard. It is not possible to determine which marketing channels are driving sales in each region or whether the South region's strength is partially a function of better campaign investment.

**Limitation 3 — Return reasons are unknown.**
The `return_flag` field identifies that a return occurred but not why. Without return reason data, it is impossible to determine whether Furniture's high return rate is a product quality issue, an expectation mismatch, or a delivery damage problem. The recommended actions are therefore directional rather than targeted.

**Limitation 4 — Customer-level view is limited.**
The dashboard aggregates to order level. There is no view of customer lifetime value, repeat purchase rate, or churn. A high-volume segment like Home Office may contain both highly loyal customers and one-time buyers — the dashboard cannot distinguish between them.

**Limitation 5 — Two-year window.**
The data covers January 2024 to December 2025. This is sufficient for trend analysis but insufficient for confirming seasonality patterns, long-term category growth trajectories, or the sustained impact of any business actions taken in late 2025.

## 8. Suggested Next Analysis

**Analysis 1 — Customer cohort analysis.**
Segment customers by their first purchase year and track retention, average order value, and return rates over time. This would reveal whether the business is retaining its best customers or relying on constant new customer acquisition to maintain revenue.

**Analysis 2 — Discount policy audit by sales team and region.**
Identify which specific team members, stores, or campaign channels are approving 21–30% discounts. This would convert the aggregate discount risk insight into a specific, actionable finding.

**Analysis 3 — Furniture cost breakdown.**
Work with the finance team to allocate logistics, return processing, and marketing costs to the Furniture category specifically. This would produce a true net margin for Furniture and strengthen the case for — or against — continued investment.

**Analysis 4 — Same Day shipping propensity model.**
Analyse which customer and order characteristics predict Same Day shipping selection. This would allow the sales team to proactively target the right customers with premium shipping promotions, rather than offering it to all customers indiscriminately.

**Analysis 5 — Regional campaign attribution.**
Map `campaign_channel` to regional sales performance to determine whether South's sales advantage correlates with higher or more effective campaign investment. This would either confirm or challenge the assumption that South's strength can be replicated in East and West through similar marketing tactics.
